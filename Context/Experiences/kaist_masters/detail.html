Foundation

Turret Motion Stabilization on Mobile Platforms

This project built the mathematical and hardware foundation for later work in vehicle controls, EKF-based estimation, and architecture-level thinking. The key point is not just “I did a thesis,” but “I closed the loop on real hardware under physical disturbance.”

Problem: The Physical Bottleneck

  • Coupled Disturbances: severe performance degradation due to unpredictable base-motion dynamics and non-linear friction in the physical hardware.
  • Sensor Noise: high-frequency noise from INS/Gyro and MTF sensors made simple PID or rule-based control mathematically insufficient for precision tracking.
  • Why this matters: the problem demanded a controller that understood the plant, not just a quick software patch.

Architecture & Math: The Core Logic

Two-Mass Dynamics Modeling

Modeled the physical plant (turret and gear structures) as a two-mass spring-damper system, then used loop-shaping to identify and isolate resonance behavior before controller tuning.

Time Delay Estimation (TDE)

Avoided heavy full-order modeling of unknown non-linear dynamics by estimating and canceling the continuous disturbance term in real time.

dtot(tk) ≈ dtot(tk-1) = τm(tk-1) − J̄ · ω̇(tk-1)

Sensor Fusion via Kalman Filter

Designed a discrete-time Kalman Filter to fuse INS/Gyro and MTF sensor data, filtering high-frequency noise and guaranteeing precision of pod velocity estimation.

x̂k|k−1 = A x̂k−1|k−1 + B uk−1

Result: Quantitative Proof

  • Successfully deployed the C/C++ control logic onto the actual physical hardware.
  • Crushed the baseline controller’s position error and achieved stable tracking close to zero steady-state error under artificial base-motion disturbance.
  • Demonstrated that the proposed logic was not just mathematically valid, but physically verified on a real system.
