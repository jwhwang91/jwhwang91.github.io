Productization

From Physics to Validated TCU Software

This project sits at the point where controls, software, and real hardware must meet. The core challenge was achieving robust shift quality without direct clutch-pressure sensing, while keeping the software viable for real-time validation and deployment.

Problem

Physical Constraints & Sensorless Environment

  • Massive inertia: controlling shifts in a 55-ton main battle tank means extreme driveline torque and high consequence for poor shift quality.
  • Hardware limitation: due to cost and durability constraints, individual clutch pressure sensors could not be installed.
  • Sensorless implication: traditional open-loop-only methods are not enough to guarantee repeatable shift quality and reliability.
  • Engineering target: recover clutch state indirectly from speed and CAN-based signals, then make the software robust enough for HILS, dynamometer, and vehicle validation.
Architecture & Math

HILS Architecture & Real-Time Integration

Real-Time HILS Integration

Bridged theoretical hydro-mechanical dynamics and software validation by integrating complex non-linear transmission plant models into the real-time HILS environment. This enabled algorithm verification before expensive dynamometer and field tests.

Plant Dynamics Utilized

Applied Newton-iteration-based lumped hydraulic models to compute solenoid-valve and clutch-piston pressure behavior while staying within real-time constraints.

Clutch Kiss-Point EOL & Adaptation Logic

Overcame the lack of direct pressure sensing by designing ASW logic that estimates the clutch kiss-point—the exact onset of torque transfer— and adapts it over time. This lets the controller transition intelligently from open-loop filling to closed-loop pressure control.

Core Technical Evidence

Performance Result & Research Output

  • Performance validation: demonstrated stronger performance than the legacy ZF TCU baseline across shift shock metrics (VDV, LFP, Zerk, Δτ), acceleration behavior, braking behavior, and steering/handling-related vehicle response.
  • Added functions beyond the legacy ZF TCU: clutch adaptation, improved EOL calibration logic, towing-start support, and fault-diagnosis information transmission.
